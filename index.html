About Me

I'm a Computer Vision and Machine Learning expert completing my Ph.D. in Electrical & Computer Engineering at UC Santa Barbara, specializing in dynamic scene-graph generation and human-object interactions.

With over 5 years of experience building scalable ML pipelines, transformer-based architectures, and synthetic 3D datasets, I've published in top-tier conferences like CVPR and WACV, consistently pushing the boundaries of what's possible in visual AI.

My research focuses on developing algorithms that can understand complex interactions between humans and objects in video, with applications ranging from autonomous systems to augmented reality.
